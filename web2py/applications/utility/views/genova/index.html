Guidelines for Tagging Classification

When you tag a photo, you assign it a keyword or phrase for simplified searching and classification. Let us go through this information session on tags that may help you with the task of tagging picture. Tags describe an image. There are multiple ways to describe an image and so naturally there are several different categories of tags. Understanding this may help you organize your thoughts later on when you are tagging pictures. The word guidelines is chosen with a purpose and when tagging actual images in this task you can rely on your own common sense, instinct or experience.

  • Foreground - Some photos have main actors in the forefront of the image (e. g. the bear in the photo on the left). Tags can identify this aspect of the image. In the images on the left chess pieces appear in the foreground of the first image while the room is in the background. In the second picture the bank appears in the foreground of the image while the trees and the park appears in the background of the image.
  • Background - Tags can describe the background of the image. Is there landscape? Is it a picture of a city? Is there sea or mountains?
  • Orientation - Tags can describe whether the image is of a portrait format or a landscape format.
  • Technique - How is the photo taken? How is the image drawn? Is it a high speed photography where you can see the climax of an action? Is it a motion blur photography where the photo was taken over a long period of time? Is it a collage created by putting together hundreds of smaller pictures?
  • Time - Was the picture taken in the evening? Is there a sunset or sunrise? Can you tell a season of the year? Do fallen leaves indicate autumn? Or do the clothes of the people indicate summer or spring?
  • Color - This may better apply to drawing rather than photos. Some drawings use shades of the same color or maybe the colors were chosen so that they show high degree of contrast? The colors may be sharp or fuzzy.
  • Emotion - Is the image evocative of certain emotion like fear anxiety or nausea? Or does it seem to have a rather calming effect on you?
  • Artistic Genre - Is the style dada? Does it belong into the movement of impressionism? Does it belong into Andy Warhol's visual art movement pop art?
