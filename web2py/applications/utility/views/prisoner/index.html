Guidelines for Writing to a Prisoner

Before you embark on this assignment we want you to learn and verify your understanding of the basic guidelines involved in this task. Incoming mail shall be disapproved if any part of it:

  1. Depicts or describes procedures for the construction of or use of weapons, ammunitions, bombs, chemical agents, or incendiary devices;
  2. Depicts, encourages, or describes methods of escape from correctional facilities or contains blueprints, drawings or similar descriptions of department of corrections facilities or institutions, or includes road maps that can facilitate escape from correctional facilities;
  3. Connotes the idea that the prisoner would suffer more punishment in the afterlife or that the punishment he is suffering is insufficient;
  4. Depicts or describes procedures for the brewing of alcoholic beverages, or the manufacture of drugs or other intoxicants;
  5. Is written in code;
  6. Depicts, describes or encourages activities which may lead to the use of physical violence or group disruption;
  7. Encourages or instructs in the commission of criminal activity;
  8. Is dangerously inflammatory in that it advocates or encourages riot, insurrection, disruption of the institution, violation of department or institution rules, the violation of which would present a serious threat to the security, order or rehabilitative objectives of the institution or the safety of any person;
  9. Threatens physical harm, blackmail or extortion;
  10. Be rude or disrespectful;
  11. Attacks people. Disagree with someone’s idea? That’s fine. Attack the person? No way;
  12. Write racial, ethnic, gender or obscene comments. Hopefully that needs no further explanation;
  13. Post anything you don’t have the right to post. That includes trademarked or private information, or personal information belonging to someone else;
  14. Discourages the prisoner from successfully continuing in his civil life upon release;
  15. Lacks enthusiasm and positive outlook;
  16. Describes an illegal activity in a positive way leading to distortion in role model creating process on the side of the prisoner;
  17. Undermines the correctionary process the prisoner is undergoing in the correction facility;
  18. Undermines the belief on the part of the prisoner that after paying his debt to the society he will not be able to become an equal member of the society;
  19. Contains criminal history, offender registration, or other personal information about another inmate or offender which, in the hands of an inmate, presents a threat to the security, order or rehabilitative objectives of the correctional system or to the safety of any person;
  20. It contains an advertisement promoting any of the following where the advertisement is the focus of, rather than being incidental to, the publication or the advertising is prominent or prevalent throughout the publication.
    • Three-way calling services;
    • Pen-pal services;
    • The purchase of products or services with postage stamps; or
    • Conducting a business or profession while incarcerated

On the other hand, the letter will receive better evaluation grade if it attempts to:

  1. persuade, or convince the prisoners, to take action to improve;
  2. convince the prisoner that increased effort and energy will lead to improved performance;
  3. provide an outline of the problem and the benefits of change which will require elements of conviction, direction, intensity and persistence from the speaker in their efforts to persuade the prisoner to take a pro-active approach;
  4. establish credibility by demonstrating expertise, evidence and knowledge of the prisoner's situtation - presenting facts, statistics or quotes together with any personal experiences;
  5. make the audience believe that there is a problem which requires solving;
  6. provide ideas and direction to solve the problem;
  7. convey the subject with enthusiasm, passion and intensity to appeal to the emotions of the prisoner;
  8. comes across as truthful and sincere. Insincere and artificial sounding talk will be obvious to the prisoner and not achieve the desired results;
  9. Humor truly is the best medicine. People love to laugh. Chances are, the letter-writers who are most memorable to you are those who conveyed their message and were able to do it with humor. Letters should contain a good bit of humor. After all, motivation is all about uplifting the prisoner, and what better way than with humor?
  10. Include practical anecdotes from your own life. The prisoners will be able to relate to you as a regular person who has been through and overcome tough times. This will help you develop an instant rapport with the prisoner. Prisoner has to be able to identify with you if you are going to create a genuine connection with them. There is nothing more inspiring for the prisoners, than to realize that the person who is writing them has actually made it through and become successful. They will get the feeling that if you can go through what you did and came out on the other side successfully they will believe they can too.
